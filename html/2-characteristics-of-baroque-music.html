2. CHARACTERISTICS OF BAROQUE MUSIC

2.1. Goodbye polyphony, hello homophony

During the Baroque period, music changed profoundly. Renaissance polyphony lost its dominant role. Composers abandoned its complexity and gave priority to a single main voice with accompaniment, a type of homophony: melody and accompaniment. This consists of a main melody accompanied by other instruments. In other words, we moved away from several independent voices singing different melodies to a main melodic line with accompaniment.

This change is very important, because most music today has a main melody with accompaniment


2.2. The basso continuo

Along with the concept of melody and accompaniment comes the idea of the basso continuo. This consists of a line of low notes that is maintained throughout the piece. It is played by instruments such as the organ, harpsichord or cello, and serves as a harmonic foundation.

2.3. Tonality (Key)

The concept of tonality became fully established: the rules of major and minor chords are established, and scales are consolidated as the basis of all Western music. We will learn about this in the section musical language. 

2.4. The measured rhythm

We abandon free time and move on to a measured rhythm, where the music acquires a regular pulse thanks to the use of bars.

2.5. Contrasts and virtuosity

Finally, Baroque music is full of contrasts: loud/soft, soloist/orchestra, low/high. These are essential for expressing emotions.
