From naturalism to excess

1. HISTORICAL CONTEXT

The Baroque period lasted from 1600 to 1750 (approximately). During this time, absolute monarchies dominated Europe, and both the Church and the nobility used art to demonstrate their power and wealth. This era saw the flourishing of painting (Velázquez, Rubens), literature (Lope de Vega, Molière) and science (Galileo, Newton). The word baroque means overdone (recargado) or ornamented (adornado), and sums up the taste for excess and emotion. The Baroque mentality combines reason and feeling: it seeks technical perfection and, at the same time, to move the audience. 

To understand the change in mindset from the Renaissance to the Baroque, look at these two works of art:

  • Left: The School of Athens by Raphael -> A balanced and calm work, with clear lines, few shadows and a sense of harmony.

  • Right: The Elevation of the Cross by Peter Paul Rubens a dramatic work, full of contrast, movement and rich details.
